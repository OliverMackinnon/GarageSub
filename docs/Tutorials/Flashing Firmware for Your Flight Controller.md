# Flashing ArduSub Onto Your Flight Controller

1.  Go to <https://firmware.ardupilot.org/Sub/stable/>. Select your
    flight controller then select the .apj file. This is the firmware
    for your flight controller.

> ![A screenshot of a computer AI-generated content may be
> incorrect.](./media/media/image1.png){width="6.5in"
> height="3.0493055555555557in"}

2.  After downloading an installing QGround Control on your computer,
    connect your flight controller to your computer via USB.

3.  After connecting you should see a screen with a map. The top left
    should be green and it should say "Ready To Fly." Click on the Q in
    the top left corner. Select "Vehicle Configuration" in the window
    that pops up.

> ![A map of the state of maine AI-generated content may be
> incorrect.](./media/media/image2.png){width="6.5in"
> height="3.7743055555555554in"}

4.  Select "Firmware" in the new screen. It is ok if you do not have the
    same exact options.

5.  Unplug and re-plug in your flight controller

![A screenshot of a computer AI-generated content may be
incorrect.](./media/media/image3.png){width="2.9585892388451445in"
height="2.083513779527559in"}

6.  Select "Advanced settings" and then select "Standad Version
    (stable)" then select "Custom firmware file..." in the drop down.
    Then press ok in the top right.

>
> You should see a green bar at the bottom of your screen. Once it
> reaches the end, your firmware should be done!
>
